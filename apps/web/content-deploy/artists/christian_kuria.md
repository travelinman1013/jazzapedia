## Biography
Christian Kuria is a Kenyan-American R&B artist, producer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist from the San Francisco Bay Area, specifically Vallejo, CA. Born and raised in the region, Kuria first attracted internet attention in 2016 when he began posting Hip Hop and R&B guitar arrangements on YouTube, showcasing his ability to blend soulful vocals with passionate guitar solos. His early success with instrumental covers led him to incorporate fully-produced arrangements that combined nostalgic soul with contemporary production elements, establishing his distinctive sound that merges vintage and modern R&B aesthetics.

Kuria's professional career accelerated in 2018 when he committed to his independent music project, releasing one song monthly (later extending to every two months) as part of his creative development. He released his debut EP 'Yearlong' in 2019, followed by his cohesive project 'Borderline' in 2020. His breakthrough came with the single 'Toroka,' released in 2020, which accumulated over 40 million streams on Spotify. In late 2019, Kuria made his US touring debut as support for critically acclaimed artist Cautious Clay, a pivotal moment that validated his artistic direction and demonstrated his growing industry recognition.

Kuria's music is characterized by soulful vocals, sincere lyrics, and skilled guitar work that distinguishes him within the contemporary R&B landscape. His artistic philosophy emphasizes authenticity and following his intuition, believing that staying true to himself is essential to connecting with listeners. With over 2.1 million listeners on Spotify, Kuria has performed at prestigious venues including The Troubadour, Mercury Lounge, and Jazz Cafe, as well as international festivals including Java Jazz Festival, Sol Blume, and Seoul Jazz Festival. His music draws heavily from his Kenyan heritage and punk rock influences, creating a unique fusion that resonates with R&B audiences globally.
