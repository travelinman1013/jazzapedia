## Biography
The Rickie Clark Company was an early 1980s boogie funk band founded in Indianapolis by Paul Thomas (also known as Pryme Tyme, 'P', or the 'Funky Skunk'), alongside Stanlee Paul. Emerging from the local music scene, Thomas began his career performing with various R&B, pop, rock, jazz, and gospel acts before transitioning to multi-track recording in 1979 and touring with the disco group LTD in 1981. Upon returning to Indianapolis in 1983, the group signed to Circle City Records, a disco and funk label that became a division of Tyscot, Inc., allowing them to release secular music parallel to the label's gospel focus[2][4].

The band's sound blended boogie funk with electro elements, characteristic of the era's dance music innovations.
