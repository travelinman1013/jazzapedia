## Biography
Little Simz (Simbiatu 'Simbi' Abisola Abiola Ajikawo) is a British rapper, singer, and actress born on February 23, 1994, in Islington, North London. She developed a passion for hip-hop at age seven after being inspired by Missy Elliott, later drawing influence from Lauryn Hill, Tupac, Nas, and Jay-Z. Beginning her career with the release of her first mixtape 'Stratosphere' in 2010 at age 16, which she recorded in her childhood bedroom and self-funded by purchasing her first microphone with money from a Saturday job at a gallery, Little Simz established herself as an independent artist through four mixtapes and multiple EPs released between 2010 and 2015.

Little Simz achieved critical acclaim with her debut studio album 'A Curious Tale of Trials + Persons' (2015), which earned a cosign from Kendrick Lamar and showcased her lyrical prowess and autobiographical songwriting approach. Her third album 'Grey Area' (2019) became her biggest success to date, earning a Mercury Prize nomination and winning Best Album at both the Ivor Novello Awards and NME Awards. Her fourth album 'Sometimes I Might Be Introvert' (2021) debuted at number 4 on the UK Albums Chart and received widespread critical acclaim, with several publications naming it the best album of 2021. The album demonstrated her versatility across jazz, afrobeat, and experimental sounds, and earned her the 2022 BRIT Award for Best New Artist and the Libera Award for Best Hip-Hop/Rap Record.
