## Biography
Aquarius Heaven, born Brian Brewster, is an electronic music artist with deep roots in reggae, dancehall, and hip hop.[1][2] Before transitioning to electronic music, Brewster established himself as a reggae dancehall and hip hop singer.[1] His entry into the electronic music world came by chance in 2005 when he relocated to Paris and met the Parisian electronic music trio dOP.[1][2] Spending extensive hours in the studio with dOP profoundly transformed his musical direction, yet his Caribbean vocal influences remained evident in his work.[1][2]

The artist officially adopted the name "Aquarius Heaven" in the summer of 2010 while recording the track "Universe" with Clement of dOP, after discovering they shared the same February birthday.[1][2] This marked the beginning of his electronic music career, which was formalized with the release of "Before U Go" on the Watergate 06 album, mixed by dOP.[1][2] His debut EP "7 Days" on Circus Company generated significant buzz in the music world, followed by the "Can't Buy Love" EP on Wolf + Lamb, which achieved notable success through music blog coverage and a music video release.[1][2] Aquarius Heaven's style is characterized by a boundary-breaking approach that blends electronic music with his reggae and hip hop heritage, creating a distinctive sound unbound by conventional genre limitations.[1][2]
