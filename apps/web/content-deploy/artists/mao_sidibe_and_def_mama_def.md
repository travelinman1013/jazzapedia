## Biography
Mao Sidibé is a Senegalese musician and dancer raised in Dakar, where he founded the dancehall crew BBC Sound System. He launched his own music and video production company, Mao Productions, in 2012 and received a nomination for the RFI Prix Découvertes in 2015. Sidibé blends reggae, pop, and dancehall with Senegalese traditional drums and instruments, as demonstrated in projects like the 2023 MSF climate initiative song 'Oya,' inspired by patient testimonies from Niger, Cameroon, and Madagascar.[4][5]

Def Mama Def (also styled as Defmaa Maadef or Def Maa Maa Def) is a Senegalese duo formed by Mamy Victory and Defa, two rising stars in the hip-hop and urban music scene. Defa, formerly of group Rafa and a backing singer for Daara J Family, is known as one of Senegal's top R&B voices, while Mamy Victory is recognized as a vocal powerhouse. Formed around 2022 by Doudou Sarr of Dakar Music Expo, the duo fuses urban sounds with traditional Senegalese elements like sabar rhythms to bridge generations and promote cultural transmission, drawing from influences such as Youssou N’Dour, Baaba Maal, and Orchestra Baobab.[1][2][3]

Their music addresses women's empowerment, as in songs like 'Jigeen' (meaning 'woman' in Wolof), 'Kalanakh,' and 'Oh Maliko,' encouraging women to claim their societal roles. Touring Senegal extensively, including Casamance and Dakar, they face challenges like limited institutional support but aim to pioneer exporting Senegalese music for the new generation with high-energy live shows.[1][3]

## Fun Facts
- Def Mama Def's duo was sparked by Doudou Sarr spotting the friendship between Defa and Mamy Victory during studio sessions, leading to their formation as 'proud black female' artists.[1]
- Mao Sidibé's 'Oya' lyrics draw directly from real MSF patient and staff testimonies in Niger, Cameroon, and Madagascar to highlight climate health impacts.[5]
- Defa moved from Saint-Louis to Dakar in 2015 after opening for a 'big diva,' seeking better opportunities for artistic expression and concerts.[1]
- The duo toured remote Senegalese areas like Casamance to immerse in sabar traditions, reshaping their view of national music during an artistic residency.[1]
