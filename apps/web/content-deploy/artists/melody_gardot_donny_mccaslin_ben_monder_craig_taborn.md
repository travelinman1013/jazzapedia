## Biography
Melody Gardot is an American jazz vocalist, guitarist, and pianist born in New Jersey in 1985[1][2]. She began taking piano lessons at age nine and by 16 was performing at local nightclubs in Philadelphia, singing a mix of jazz, pop, and rock cover songs[1]. Her early years were marked by financial hardship, as she spent much of her youth living with her Polish immigrant grandmother in Philadelphia while her mother worked as a photographer[1].

At 19 years old, while studying fashion at the Community College of Philadelphia, Gardot's life changed dramatically when she was struck by an SUV during an illegal turn while riding her bicycle[1][2]. The accident left her with serious injuries including head trauma and pelvic fractures, confining her to a hospital bed for a year and requiring extensive physical therapy[1][7]. The injuries resulted in increased sensitivity to light and sound, prompting her to gravitate toward softer musical genres including bossa nova, jazz, French chanson, and folk music[1]. A physician suggested that music could aid her cognitive recovery, leading her to teach herself guitar and begin writing songs as personal therapy[6].

Gardot's professional career began in 2005 with the EP *Some Lessons: The Bedroom Sessions*, recorded from her bedside[5]. She achieved critical acclaim with her 2007 debut album *Worrisome Heart* on Verve Records, which peaked at number two on Billboard's Traditional Jazz Albums chart[1]. Known for her dusky, delicately nuanced vocal style and sophisticated interpretations of standards and original compositions, Gardot has built a loyal international fan base[1]. She has released six studio albums including *My One and Only Thrill* (2009), *The Absence* (2012), *Currency of Man* (2015), *Sunset in the Blue* (2020), and *Entre Eux Deux* (2022), earning five Top Ten Billboard Traditional Jazz Albums and two number ones[1]. Her work has drawn comparisons to Joni Mitchell, Laura Nyro, and Nina Simone[1].
