## Biography
Meriheini Luoto is a Finnish composer and performer based in Helsinki, specializing in avant-garde folk music on violin, nyckelharpa, electronics, and vocals. She began playing classical violin at age four in a small town in Southern Finland and discovered her passion for ensemble playing through early folk music courses like Näppäri, where she encountered group performances with hundreds of young players. Continuing through Sibelius Upper Secondary School and the Sibelius Academy's Junior Academy, she earned an M.Mus. in folk music, forming key ensembles like the contemporary folk group Hohka, which was active for 11 years, and embracing experimental approaches including free improvisation, singing, and music technology.[1][2][3]

## Fun Facts
- Once played a bicycle as a musical instrument on a TV show, inspired by her junior high music teacher's experimental ideas.[3]
- Her debut album Metsänpeitto (2017) used binaural recording to create a 3D sound sensation mimicking forest cover folklore, nominated for the Teosto Prize.[1][2]
- Listens to metal and hard rock as a teenager, which subtly influences her fierce, rough sound despite her folk roots.[3]
- Creates music imagining the isolation of ancient pelimanni folk players, composing from improvisations derived from forest soundscapes.[2]
