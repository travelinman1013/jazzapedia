## Biography
Choi Dong-wook, known by his stage name Seven (often stylized as 7een), was born on November 9, 1984, in South Korea. He began training under YG Entertainment at the age of fifteen, undergoing four years of intensive singing and dancing preparation before debuting in 2003 with his first album *Just Listen*, released on March 8. This debut earned him multiple best new artist awards, including at the Golden Disc Awards, MBC Gayo Daejejeon, Mnet Music Video Festival, SBS Gayo Daejeon, and Seoul Music Awards.[1]

Seven's career rapidly expanded with his second album *Must Listen* in 2004, which topped the Recording Industry Association of Korea chart and featured the hit 'Passion' that won several awards. He achieved pan-Asian success, promoting in Thailand where the album sold over 100,000 copies, and released his first Chinese album in 2006. His third Korean album *24/Se7en* and Japanese debut *First Se7en* both charted highly, followed by *Se7olution* later that year. Seven attempted a U.S. breakthrough with English single 'Girls' featuring Lil' Kim in 2009, becoming the first Asian artist on BET's *106 & Park*, though plans were disrupted by leaks. His style blends R&B, pop, and hip-hop influences with mature, passionate themes.[1]

After a hiatus and leaving YG, Seven returned independently in 2016 with 'I'm Good' and mini-album *I Am Se7en*, featuring 'Give It to Me'. He signed with Dmost Entertainment in 2020 for further promotions. Despite challenges like military service and label changes, Seven established himself as a Hallyu star with a legacy of chart-topping albums and cross-cultural breakthroughs, though his Spotify popularity remains low at 1.[1]

## Fun Facts
- Seven's U.S. debut single 'This Is My Year' featuring Fabolous leaked on YouTube in 2007, forcing YG Entertainment to revise plans.[1]
- He became the first Asian artist to have a music video ('Girls') played on BET's *106 & Park* in 2009.[1]
- 'Must Listen' sold over 100,000 copies in Thailand, earning him Popular Asian Artist at Channel V Thailand Music Video Awards.[1]
- His 2012 track 'When I Can't Sing' marked the first official collaboration between YG and JYP Entertainment.[1]
