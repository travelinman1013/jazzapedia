## Biography
Florence Welch, born on 28 August 1986 in London, England, is the lead vocalist and primary songwriter of the indie rock band Florence + the Machine, formed in 2007 as a collaboration with keyboardist Isabella 'Isa Machine' Summers. An art college dropout from Camberwell, Welch was discovered singing Motown covers, including Etta James' 'Something’s Got a Hold on Me,' in a nightclub restroom, which led to her manager and early opportunities. She initially performed with Summers under the name Florence Robot & Isa Machine in small London venues, evolving from a private joke into the band's name after Welch shortened it for her first gig. Prior to the band's debut, Welch recorded with the group Ashok, releasing an album titled *Plans* that included an early version of 'Kiss with a Fist.'[1][2][3][5][6]

## Fun Facts
- The band name 'Florence + the Machine' originated as a private joke between Florence Welch and Isabella Summers, starting as 'Florence Robot & Isa Machine' before being shortened just before Welch's first gig because the full name was too long.
- Welch was discovered drunk in a nightclub restroom singing an Etta James cover, which immediately won her a manager and launched her career.
- Welch's mother is a professor of Renaissance Studies, and her father worked in advertising with arts involvement, inspiring her romantic, dramatic performance style reminiscent of Renaissance artists.
- The band's debut album *Lungs* (2009) sold over 100,000 copies in its first weeks and held the UK number 2 spot for five consecutive weeks.
