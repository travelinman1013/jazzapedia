## Biography
Mitch Dalton is a highly accomplished UK-based guitarist renowned as one of the most in-demand studio musicians in the country. He has built an extensive career playing on numerous hit albums and smash singles for major artists, as well as contributing to countless soundtracks. His versatility allows him to excel across a wide range of styles, drawing from American musical genres including fusion, Latin jazz, and bebop[1][2][6].

Dalton's professional journey highlights his status as a premier session player, having worked with high-profile names such as Robbie Williams, Marc Almond, and Ella Fitzgerald, among others. In addition to his session work, he leads his own project, Mitch Dalton & The Studio Kings, with whom he released the album 'Meet Mitch Dalton and the Studio Kings' on the Regius label. This record features premier instrumental cuts showcasing his masterful guitar playing alongside a consummate band, including collaborations with keyboardist David Arch[1][3][5].

Dalton's legacy lies in his flawless execution across diverse musical landscapes, from pop hits to jazz-infused explorations, cementing his reputation as a go-to guitarist for top-tier productions and live performances[2][5][6].
