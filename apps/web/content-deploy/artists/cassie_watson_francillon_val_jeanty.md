## Biography
Cassie Watson Francillon, also known as Cassandra Watson Francillon, is a visionary harpist and interdisciplinary artist based in New Orleans, Louisiana. She constructs a 'sacred sonic architecture' through modern music on her concert grand harp, influenced by hip-hop, jazz, psychedelia, Black American spirituals, and her father's Haitian lineage. Her work reflects the journey, joy, and lamentations of modern Black American transcendence, exploring the vibratory essence of language in music as a healing and transformative act. She has shared her music extensively in New York, North, Central and South America, Europe, and Haiti, with her solo debut album *hOULe* (Gold Bolus Recordings) receiving international acclaim.[3][5]

## Fun Facts
- Cassie Watson Francillon is part of a 'New Orleans Haitian gumbo' electroacoustic duet with Val Jeanty, channeling specific cultural elements in their experimental music.[4]
- Her recent release Bardo (self-released) follows duo albums Suite with Sasha Masakowski and KāFOU with Val Jeanty, marking her third album in 11 months.[6]
- She has performed in events like Reimagining Tropiques and KāFOU at Greenwich House Recital Hall, blending harp with electronics in avant-garde contexts.[3]
