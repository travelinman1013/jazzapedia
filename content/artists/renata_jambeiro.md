## Biography
Renata Jambeiro is a Brazilian singer, composer, and actress born on December 22, 1981, in Brasília[2]. She has established herself as one of the prominent voices of the new generation of Brazilian samba artists[1]. Her career took shape in the 2000s when she began releasing her musical works, starting with her debut album "Jambeiro" in 2007[1].

Jambeiro's artistic approach is characterized by a fusion of traditional and modern samba styles infused with African influences[1]. In 2009, she released the DVD "Sambaluaye," which explores and chronicles the African influence in Brazilian culture[1]. Her performances are noted for their distinctive musicality, blending different samba styles with the African rhythmic elements that define her sound. She presents both classics of Brazilian music and original compositions from her recordings[1].

Throughout her career, Jambeiro has been recognized as an artist with exceptional performance abilities, charisma, and talent[1]. Her work contributes to the contemporary Brazilian music landscape, particularly within the samba and axé genres[3].
